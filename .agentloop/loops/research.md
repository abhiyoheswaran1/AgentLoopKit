# Research Loop

Use this loop when planning or summarizing local product, customer, usability, or technical research.

Required inputs:

- Research question
- Target audience or system context
- Source of evidence
- Constraints, consent, or privacy boundaries
- Decision the research should inform

Process:

1. Define the research question and decision boundary.
2. List the evidence source: interviews, notes, support tickets, analytics exports, repo artifacts, or internal simulations.
3. Mark simulated persona output as decision support, not real user evidence.
4. Capture findings with confidence, source, and limits.
5. Turn accepted findings into scoped follow-up task contracts.

Acceptance criteria:

- Research question and decision boundary are explicit.
- Findings distinguish observed evidence from assumptions or simulations.
- Follow-up recommendations are scoped and testable.

Verification:

- Check source links or local artifact paths.
- Confirm private or sensitive data is not copied into public docs.
- Run relevant docs or link checks when publishing research notes.

Handoff output:

- Research question
- Evidence source and limits
- Findings
- Recommended next tasks
- Non-goals and open questions
